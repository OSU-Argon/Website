      # Research Team


      ## About Us


      The <Superscript value="40" />Ar/<Superscript value="39" />Ar
      geochronology method has had a profound impact on the Earth system
      sciences since its introduction in 1965. Here in the **[Oregon State
      University Argon Geochronology Lab](\ "Oregon State University Argon
      Geochronology Lab")** (OSU-ARGON) we have been employing this dating
      method ever since 1977 with a focus on volcanism in both the marine and
      terrestrial environment to improve the geochronology of the ocean crust,
      ocean island volcanism, large igneous provinces, lunar and planetary
      rocks, hydrothermal minerals and clays, ore deposits and critical
      minerals, detrital mica and feldspar minerals, and so on â€¦

      ## Staff


      This NSF-sponsored facility is currently directed by Prof. [Anthony
      Koppers](http://ceoas.oregonstate.edu/profile/koppers/ "Anthony Koppers")
      and open to visiting scientists, post-docs, and students from across and
      outside the USA. Together with predecessor Emeritus Prof. [Bob
      Duncan](http://ceoas.oregonstate.edu/profile/duncan/ "Bob Duncan") they
      have built up strong research programs with an emphasis on the
      geochronology of ocean basin volcanism and mantle geodynamics, including
      the dating of the ocean floor, seamounts, volcanic islands and large
      igneous provinces.
